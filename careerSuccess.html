Career Success

On May 27, 2015, Tesfaye revealed a new song, “The Hills,” which would eventually become the second single on Beauty Behind the Madness. The song debuted at number twenty on the Billboard Hot 100 and peaked at number one. A later release on June 8, “Can't Feel My Face,” continued his string of successful singles and also topped the Billboard Hot 100 charts. Tesfaye's second studio album Beauty Behind the Madness came out on August 28 and immediately saw success: it debuted at number one on the Billboard 200; charted in the top ten among several other countries; and eventually, it would win a Grammy for Best Urban Contemporary Album. To promote the album, Tesfaye headlined at various summer music festivals and kicked off The Madness Fall Tour, his first large-scale tour in U.S. arenas.

Leading up to his third studio album, Tesfaye continued to collaborate with artists: he featured on Travis Scott's debut album Rodeo, on Kanye West's seventh studio album The Life of Pablo, on Future's Evol, and on Beyonce's Lemonade album. In September 2016, Tesfaye announced his newest album Starboy, to be released on November 25. The title track “Starboy,” which featured electronic music duo Daft Punk, was released ahead of the album on September 21 and once again, peaked at number one on the Billboard Hot 100; a second collaboration with Daft Punk, “I Feel It Coming,” was released on November 24. Starboy debuted as number one on the Billboard 200 as well—becoming his second consecutive number-one album—and since its release, it has accumulated over 150 million streams and has been certified triple-platinum by the RIAA. It also won Tesfaye his second Grammy win for Best Urban Contemporary Album with prominent features such as Kendrick Lamar, Lana Del Ray, and Future. In support of the album, Tesfaye began his tour, Starboy: Legend of the Fall Tour, in February 2017. He visited cities across Europe, North America, South America, and Oceania, and concluded the tour in December.
